Introduction

The Wendigo is a creature from Algonquian folklore representing insatiable hunger and spiritual corruption. Stories describe a tall skeletal monster that stalks the frozen forests of North America.

Historical Origins

The legend comes from Indigenous cultures including the Ojibwe, Cree, and Algonquin peoples. The stories warned against greed and cannibalism during harsh winters.

Mythology

Transformation Process

A human may become a Wendigo through cannibalism or spiritual possession. The transformation represents the loss of humanity.

Powers

The Wendigo is said to possess supernatural speed, strength, and endless hunger.

Modern Cultural Impact

The Wendigo appears in films, literature, and games. However, for Indigenous communities it remains a powerful moral teaching rather than simply a horror monster.
